## Scope

This topic covers models in which *beliefs and anticipation enter the utility function directly*: an agent savors or dreads a still-unresolved future, derives "ego utility" or self-image from flattering self-views, and therefore demands, distorts, or avoids information for non-instrumental reasons. The boundary is the belief-as-carrier-of-utility primitive — present felicity depends on what one expects or remembers, not only on realized consumption. It thus excludes purely instrumental information economics and purely outcome-based reference dependence (those border the [[expectations-based-reference-dependence]] and [[disappointment-regret-nonexpected-utility]] topics), and it includes the neuroscience of dread and the experienced-utility tradition only insofar as they ground the anticipatory channel.

## Sub-themes

- **Founding the anticipatory channel.** [[@Loewenstein1987|Loewenstein (1987)]] and [[@Loewenstein1992|Loewenstein & Elster (1992)]] put savoring/dread into deterministic intertemporal utility; [[@Caplin2001|Caplin & Leahy (2001)]] extend it to uncertainty (anxiety, psychological expected utility); [[@Koszegi2010|Kőszegi (2010)]] generalizes via personal equilibrium when anticipation *interacts* with outcomes; [[@Sarver2012|Sarver (2012)]] gives an axiomatic recursive representation.
- **Motivated / optimal beliefs as a chosen asset.** [[@Brunnermeier2005|Brunnermeier & Parker (2005)]] and [[@Brunnermeier2007|Brunnermeier, Gollier & Parker (2007)]] choose priors to maximize well-being (optimism, skewness preference); [[@Benabou2009|Bénabou & Tirole (2009)]] and [[@Benabou2011|Bénabou & Tirole (2011)]] make beliefs assets sustained by self-signaling; [[@Koszegi2006a|Kőszegi (2006a)]] (ego utility) and [[@Koszegi2022|Kőszegi, Loewenstein & Murooka (2022)]] (fragile self-esteem) and [[@Huffman2022|Huffman, Raymond & Shvets (2022)]] (field overconfidence + biased memory) build the self-image strand.
- **Beliefs disciplined by ex-post pain.** [[@Chen2013|Chen (2013)]], [[@Gollier2010|Gollier & Muermann (2010)]] and [[@Jouini2014|Jouini, Karehnke & Napp (2014)]] add loss aversion/disappointment so optimism is bounded — savoring versus disappointment.
- **Information preferences, avoidance, and disclosure.** [[@Oster2013a|Oster, Shoulson & Dorsey (2013)]] / [[@Oster2016|Oster et al. (2016)]] (Huntington testing), [[@Falk2016|Falk & Zimmermann (2016)]] and [[@Ganguly2017|Ganguly & Tasoff (2017)]] (lab info-demand), [[@Koszegi2006|Kőszegi (2006b)]] (emotional agency / strategic disclosure), and [[@Matthey2008|Matthey (2008)]] (utility from expectations).
- **Hedonic measurement and neural substrates.** [[@Kahneman1997|Kahneman, Wakker & Sarin (1997)]] and [[@Baucells2017|Baucells & Bellezza (2017)]] (experienced/total utility, anticipation-event-recall); [[@Loewenstein2001a|Loewenstein et al. (2001)]] (risk-as-feelings); [[@Berns2006|Berns et al. (2006)]], [[@Berns2007|Berns, Laibson & Loewenstein (2007)]], [[@Mobbs2007|Mobbs et al. (2007)]], [[@Schmitz2012|Schmitz & Grillon (2012)]] (neural/psychophysiology of dread and threat imminence).

## Cross-paper tensions

**The sharpest tension is the direction of belief distortion and the demand for information.** The optimal-expectations program ([[@Brunnermeier2005|Brunnermeier & Parker]], [[@Brunnermeier2007|Brunnermeier-Gollier-Parker]]) predicts *uniform over-optimism* and therefore a preference to *delay* resolution ($\sigma_L \succ \sigma_S$) so the dream can be savored. The disappointment-disciplined models flip this: once a self-chosen optimistic reference point raises the pain of a shortfall ([[@Gollier2010|Gollier & Muermann]], [[@Chen2013|Chen]]), agents become *more* risk-averse, can be over-*pessimistic*, and — in [[@Chen2013|Chen (2013)]] — optimism vs. pessimism switches at a single cutoff $P^*=\frac{\eta\lambda-1}{\eta(\lambda-1)}$ in loss-aversion $\lambda$ and future weight $\eta$. The experiments adjudicate *against* uniform optimism: [[@Falk2016|Falk & Zimmermann (2016)]] find 76% prefer information *sooner* (contradicting Brunnermeier-Parker), while [[@Ganguly2017|Ganguly & Tasoff (2017)]] find info-demand *scales with* expected consumption utility (fantasy vs. dread). [[@Falk2016|Falk & Zimmermann]] further show the sign reverses with *attention* — when distraction is available, later news becomes attractive — a channel neither the optimal-belief nor the disappointment models contain.

**Rival belief-formation primitives.** Three incompatible accounts of where beliefs come from coexist: (i) *chosen priors* maximizing well-being ([[@Brunnermeier2005|Brunnermeier & Parker]]), (ii) *Bayesian* updating with bias entering only through endogenous information-stopping ([[@Koszegi2006a|Kőszegi (2006a)]]), and (iii) *mood-congruent memory* sampling ([[@Koszegi2022|Kőszegi-Loewenstein-Murooka]], [[@Huffman2022|Huffman et al.]], [[@Benabou2011|Bénabou-Tirole]]). These are not observationally innocent: [[@Koszegi2010|Kőszegi (2010)]] proves a tight *observational-equivalence* result — an anticipatory agent is indistinguishable from expected utility unless she displays self-fulfilling expectations, endogenous time-inconsistency, or informational preferences — and notes that rationality (chosen *rational* beliefs) sits uneasily with the motivated-belief models. [[@Ganguly2017|Ganguly & Tasoff]] cite an Eliaz-Spiegler impossibility: Bayesian [[@Caplin2001|Caplin-Leahy]] anticipatory utility *cannot* generate their information-consumption complementarity, so the data force either non-Bayesian updating or attention.

**Menu-dependence and stochastic dominance.** [[@Gollier2010|Gollier & Muermann]] deliberately build a *menu-independent* criterion respecting second-degree stochastic dominance, faulting [[@Brunnermeier2005|Brunnermeier & Parker]] (where welfare can fall as the choice set grows). [[@Jouini2014|Jouini et al.]] then show that endogenizing the admissible-belief set to the lottery's support reintroduces a "support effect" and can *violate* FSD/SSD unless savoring weight is bounded — so tractability, dominance, and menu-independence cannot be had simultaneously.

**Welfare sign.** [[@Benabou2011|Bénabou & Tirole (2011)]] make the verdict hinge on whether beliefs are *hedonic* assets (malleability always lowers welfare; "treadmill effect") or *self-control* devices (malleability can raise welfare) — the *same* positive behavior, opposite normative conclusions, also echoed in [[@Benabou2009|Bénabou-Tirole (2009)]]'s dignity-bargaining impasse and in [[@Oster2013a|Oster et al.]]'s point that forced testing can be welfare-reducing.

**Is dread emotion or attention?** [[@Berns2006|Berns et al. (2006)]] locate dread in *posterior* (attentive/somatosensory) pain-matrix regions, not anterior "emotional" cortex, and [[@Mobbs2007|Mobbs et al.]] show a forebrain-to-midbrain shift with imminence — challenging the purely affective "risk-as-feelings" reading of [[@Loewenstein2001a|Loewenstein et al. (2001)]] and pointing toward attention-based anticipatory models.

## Open questions

- **What disciplines belief distortion?** Every chosen-belief model flags its "frictionless" prior: [[@Brunnermeier2005|Brunnermeier & Parker]] leave belief-distortion costs/non-detectability open; [[@Gollier2010|Gollier & Muermann]] and [[@Jouini2014|Jouini et al.]] pin down only a scalar reference $y^*$, not a unique distribution.
- **Bayes vs. utility-serving updating.** [[@Chen2013|Chen]] notes Bayes "can hardly hold" when beliefs are utility-serving; [[@Koszegi2010|Kőszegi]] and [[@Ganguly2017|Ganguly-Tasoff]] both leave a *utility-consistent updating rule* unspecified.
- **Dynamics.** [[@Gollier2010|Gollier & Muermann]] and [[@Koszegi2010|Kőszegi]] explicitly call for *dynamic* optimal-belief paths; [[@Sarver2012|Sarver]] leaves uniqueness/continuity of the recursive value and a full calibration open.
- **Positive vs. negative domain and external validity.** [[@Falk2016|Falk & Zimmermann]] tested only an aversive event (no validated positive lab stimulus); [[@Ganguly2017|Ganguly-Tasoff]] could not causally move affect; [[@Oster2013a|Oster et al.]] / [[@Oster2016|the corrigendum]] cannot separate motivated belief from denial/coping and revised the magnitude of bias downward.
- **Endogenous stakes and memory structure.** [[@Koszegi2022|Kőszegi-Loewenstein-Murooka]] leave the importance-of-success $k$ exogenous and the self-view scalar; [[@Huffman2022|Huffman et al.]]'s biased-memory model still under-predicts the *prevalence* of bias.

## Candidate ideas

- **Adjudicate the sooner/later split structurally.** Re-run the [[@Falk2016|Falk & Zimmermann (2016)]] information-timing design with a validated *positive* stimulus and an attention-availability arm, and structurally estimate the [[@Chen2013|Chen (2013)]] cutoff $P^*$ to test whether optimism/pessimism switches with elicited $\lambda$ — distinguishing [[@Brunnermeier2005|Brunnermeier-Parker]] from [[@Gollier2010|Gollier-Muermann]].
- **A utility-consistent updating rule.** Build and axiomatize the non-Bayesian updating that [[@Chen2013|Chen]], [[@Koszegi2010|Kőszegi (2010)]] and [[@Ganguly2017|Ganguly-Tasoff]] all flag as missing, nesting Bayesian [[@Caplin2001|Caplin-Leahy]] as the no-motivation limit and testing it against the information-consumption complementarity.
- **Attention vs. affect in dread.** Pair the [[@Falk2016|Falk-Zimmermann]] distraction manipulation with the [[@Berns2006|Berns et al. (2006)]] / [[@Mobbs2007|Mobbs et al.]] imminence paradigms to test whether anticipatory disutility is attention-modulated (predicting a measurable shift in info-demand and posterior pain-matrix activity).
- **Endogenize the stake $k$ in fragile self-esteem.** Extend [[@Koszegi2022|Kőszegi-Loewenstein-Murooka (2022)]] to let agents choose domain importance (Steele "disidentification"), then test the prediction in [[@Huffman2022|Huffman et al. (2022)]]'s manager panel by linking biased recall to self-selected high-stakes domains.
- **Disclosure design under motivated principals.** Combine [[@Koszegi2006|Kőszegi (2006b)]] emotional agency with a *naive* (rather than sophisticated) principal and [[@Oster2013a|Oster et al.]]-style information avoidance to derive optimal medical-test framing — when does salience-of-consequences beat cost reduction at raising testing?
- **Menu-independence vs. stochastic dominance frontier.** Formally characterize the parameter region where the [[@Jouini2014|Jouini et al. (2014)]] endogenous-support criterion respects FSD/SSD, mapping the trade-off [[@Gollier2010|Gollier-Muermann]] and [[@Brunnermeier2005|Brunnermeier-Parker]] sit on opposite sides of.
