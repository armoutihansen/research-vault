## Scope

This topic covers how *mission match*, meaningful work, empowerment, and the intrinsic motivation of "motivated agents" reshape incentive design in organizations — and the flip side, how extrinsic rewards and control can *crowd out* the very motivation they target. The members span foundational contracting theory (mission as a substitute for, or complement to, monetary pay), the information-economic microfoundations of crowding-out, and the experimental and field evidence that disciplines both. The boundary is the *worker's* non-pecuniary stake in the task itself (mission, autonomy, esteem, reciprocity-toward-the-principal); pure distributional fairness, image-toward-third-parties, and identity sit in neighbouring topics.

## Sub-themes

- **Mission as a contractual lever (substitution vs. complementarity).** [[@Besley2005|Besley & Ghatak (2005)]] establishes the canonical "selection substitutes for incentives" benchmark with exogenous missions and assortative matching; [[@Cassar2020|Cassar & Armouti-Hansen (2020)]] endogenizes the mission as a screening/incentive instrument; [[@Besley2017|Besley & Ghatak (2017)]] makes mission *flexibility* the organizational-form choice (FP/NP/SE) and reverses the substitution logic. [[@Delfgaauw2016|Delfgaauw & Swank (2016)]] uses mission/vision as a commitment device protecting task-specific human capital.
- **Job design as a meaning technology.** [[@Cassar2018|Cassar & Meier (2018)]] supplies the unifying $U = Y(w,e) + M(\theta,x,e) - C(e)$ scaffold (mission, autonomy, competence, relatedness); [[@Beckmann2022|Beckmann & Kräkel (2022)]] operationalizes one dimension — empowerment → task commitment — and tests it on linked employer–employee data.
- **The empirical magnitude of mission.** [[@Carpenter2016|Carpenter & Gong (2016)]] is the clean real-effort estimate of the pure match effect purged of selection.
- **Microfoundations of crowding-out.** Three rival mechanisms: signaling about the principal's *information* ([[@Sliwka2007|Sliwka (2007)]], [[@Danilov2016|Danilov & Sliwka (2016)]]) or *type* ([[@Benabou2003|Bénabou & Tirole (2003)]], [[@Ellingsen2008|Ellingsen & Johannesson (2008)]]); intention-based reciprocity *without* signaling ([[@vonSiemens2013|von Siemens (2013)]]); and the canonical incomplete-contract field demonstration ([[@Gneezy2000|Gneezy & Rustichini (2000)]]).
- **Altruistic workers in markets.** [[@Bardey2021|Bardey & Siciliani (2021)]] embeds worker altruism in a two-sided nursing-home market, showing motivation reshapes the *distribution* of rents, not just effort.

## Cross-paper tensions

**Is motivation a substitute or a complement for incentive pay?** This is the sharpest fault line. [[@Besley2005|Besley & Ghatak (2005)]] makes motivation a *perfect substitute* — $e_{ij}=b_{ij}+\theta_{ij}$, so more motivated agents get lower-powered pay. [[@Carpenter2016|Carpenter & Gong (2016)]] confirms substitution empirically (piece rates repair ~68% of the mismatch deficit) and, crucially, finds **no crowding-out** of matched workers — directly contradicting the central prediction of [[@Benabou2003|Bénabou & Tirole (2003)]]. Yet [[@Besley2017|Besley & Ghatak (2017)]] *reverses their own 2005 logic*: because motivation relaxes the mission-integrity constraint, higher $\gamma_M$ *permits higher-powered* financial incentives. [[@Cassar2018|Cassar & Meier (2018)]] flatly declares the substitute-vs-complement question unresolved, noting $M$ may depend on $w$. The members thus do not agree on the sign of $\partial b^*/\partial\theta$.

**What mechanism drives crowding-out — if it exists at all?** Within the crowding-out camp, the members are rival, mutually-distinguishing models of the *same* Falk–Kosfeld and Gneezy–Rustichini facts. [[@Benabou2003|Bénabou & Tirole (2003)]] needs the principal to hold payoff-relevant private information plus a sorting condition. [[@Ellingsen2008|Ellingsen & Johannesson (2008)]] needs audience-dependent esteem ($\sigma_H>\sigma_L$). [[@Sliwka2007|Sliwka (2007)]] and [[@Danilov2016|Danilov & Sliwka (2016)]] need conformists plus the principal's superior information about the *norm*. [[@vonSiemens2013|von Siemens (2013)]] pointedly dispenses with signaling entirely: intention-based reciprocity with private types reproduces the hidden costs of control, and is *empirically distinguishable* — signaling accounts should weaken in anonymous labs, the reciprocity channel should not. [[@Gneezy2000|Gneezy & Rustichini (2000)]] concede they cannot even adjudicate between their own differential-information and social-norm interpretations. The identification problem is live and unresolved.

**Exogenous vs. endogenous mission.** [[@Besley2005|Besley & Ghatak (2005)]] takes $\theta_{ij}$ as given and explicitly flags that whether motivation is crowded in/out by the principal's actions is *outside* the model — precisely the gap [[@Benabou2003|Bénabou & Tirole (2003)]] fills. [[@Cassar2020|Cassar & Armouti-Hansen (2020)]] endogenizes the mission and shows the direction of distortion flips with the friction: non-contractible effort pushes the mission *toward* the agent (incentive device), private information about drive pushes it *toward* the principal (rent extraction). [[@Delfgaauw2016|Delfgaauw & Swank (2016)]] adds a wrinkle missing elsewhere: mission only works through *internalization rate* $\alpha$, and must be "credible and irreversible" — an assumption none of the matching models justify.

## Open questions

- **Structure of the meaning function $M(\theta,x,e)$.** Are mission, autonomy, competence, relatedness complements or substitutes ([[@Cassar2018|Cassar & Meier (2018)]])? Is pay a substitute or complement to meaning, and are monetary incentives still effective in already-high-meaning environments?
- **Which crowding-out mechanism governs a given setting?** Signaling-about-type vs. signaling-about-norm vs. reciprocity vs. esteem are not cleanly separated empirically ([[@vonSiemens2013|von Siemens (2013)]], [[@Ellingsen2008|Ellingsen & Johannesson (2008)]], [[@Danilov2016|Danilov & Sliwka (2016)]], [[@Gneezy2000|Gneezy & Rustichini (2000)]]). [[@Beckmann2022|Beckmann & Kräkel (2022)]] cannot tell whether empowerment works via ownership, reciprocity, or control aversion, nor separate hidden action from hidden information.
- **Selection and motivation jointly.** [[@Carpenter2016|Carpenter & Gong (2016)]] removes selection by design; how sorting interacts with the motivational effect in equilibrium staffing is open. [[@Besley2017|Besley & Ghatak (2017)]] flags that stable matching does not guarantee self-selection under concealed types.
- **Determinants of heterogeneity in $\theta$ / drive.** The deep sources of who values meaning remain unidentified ([[@Cassar2018|Cassar & Meier (2018)]]); richer multidimensional drive (ability × motivation) is unmodeled ([[@Cassar2020|Cassar & Armouti-Hansen (2020)]]).
- **Mission drift and financing.** Whether equity finance erodes the profit–purpose balance over time is explicitly open ([[@Besley2017|Besley & Ghatak (2017)]]); [[@Besley2005|Besley & Ghatak (2005)]] leaves bureaucratic conservatism's dynamics informal.

## Candidate ideas

- **A horse-race experiment that separates the crowding-out mechanisms.** Design a control/contract game varying principal anonymity and principal type-informativeness orthogonally, so signaling-about-norm ([[@Danilov2016|Danilov & Sliwka (2016)]]), esteem ([[@Ellingsen2008|Ellingsen & Johannesson (2008)]]), and signaling-free reciprocity ([[@vonSiemens2013|von Siemens (2013)]]) make divergent predictions.
- **Structurally estimate the meaning function $M(\cdot)$.** Use a real-effort design crossing mission, autonomy, competence, and relatedness to test complementarity in $M$ ([[@Cassar2018|Cassar & Meier (2018)]]) and recover whether pay enters $M$, settling the substitute-vs-complement tension between [[@Besley2005|Besley & Ghatak (2005)]] and [[@Besley2017|Besley & Ghatak (2017)]].
- **Endogenous-mission scoring auctions in the field.** Test [[@Cassar2020|Cassar & Armouti-Hansen (2020)]]'s prediction that missions move *toward* the agent under non-contractible effort but *toward* the principal under private drive, using aid-procurement or research-grant data where design weight is observable.
- **Joint selection + motivation estimation.** Extend [[@Carpenter2016|Carpenter & Gong (2016)]] by letting workers self-select into missions, identifying the sorting margin [[@Besley2005|Besley & Ghatak (2005)]] assumes away and [[@Besley2017|Besley & Ghatak (2017)]] flags as unproven under concealed types.
- **Empowerment microfoundation test.** Build on [[@Beckmann2022|Beckmann & Kräkel (2022)]] with a design that separates psychological ownership, reciprocity, and control aversion as the empowerment→commitment channel, and identifies hidden action vs. hidden information via menu take-up.
- **Distributional incidence of worker altruism.** Take [[@Bardey2021|Bardey & Siciliani (2021)]]'s counterintuitive result — higher altruism *raises* nurse wages — to data on staffing, prices, and pay-for-performance to test who actually captures the rents from motivated workers.
