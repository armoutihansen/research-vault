## Scope

This topic covers the Kőszegi–Rabin (KR) program in which the reference point is the decision-maker's recent *rational expectations* about outcomes, pinned down endogenously as a personal-equilibrium fixed point, together with the theory, lab, and field-data tests it has spawned. It spans the founding model and its risk and dynamic extensions, the personal-equilibrium solution concepts (PE / PPE / CPE), revealed-preference axiomatizations of their choice content, and applications to labor supply, the endowment effect, contracting, and pricing. The boundary: status-quo loss aversion and exogenous-reference prospect theory sit in the neighboring *prospect-theory-loss-aversion* topic; the transitive disappointment/regret precursors live in *disappointment-regret-nonexpected-utility*. What unifies the members here is the claim that the referent is *endogenous to expectations* — and the fight over whether that claim leaves an observable footprint.

## Sub-themes

- **Founding theory and its extensions.** [[@Koszegi2006b|Kőszegi & Rabin (2006)]] (the portable model, attachment/comparison effects, target-income labor supply), [[@Koszegi2007|Kőszegi & Rabin (2007)]] (reference-dependent *risk* attitudes, first-order risk aversion), [[@Koszegi2009|Kőszegi & Rabin (2009)]] (dynamic news utility, precautionary savings); the equilibrium-fixed-point ancestor is [[@Shalev2000|Shalev (2000)]].
- **Real-effort lab tests.** [[@Abeler2011|Abeler et al. (2011)]] (effort rises with expected pay, clusters at $f$) versus its falsifying sequel [[@Gneezy2017|Gneezy et al. (2017)]] (nonmonotone, U-shaped effort).
- **Field labor supply.** [[@Farber2005|Farber (2005)]] (hours, not income, drive stopping), [[@Farber2008|Farber (2008)]] (reference income too unstable to matter), [[@Crawford2011|Crawford & Meng (2011)]] (rational-expectations targets in *both* hours and income reconcile the puzzle).
- **Risk-elicitation and endowment-effect tests.** [[@Sprenger2015|Sprenger (2015)]] (endowment effect for risk separates KR from disappointment aversion), [[@Cerulli-Harms2019|Cerulli-Harms et al. (2019)]] (probabilistic forced exchange), [[@Baillon2020|Baillon et al. (2020)]] (which referent rule do people actually use?).
- **Decision-theoretic foundations.** [[@Freeman2016|Freeman (2016)]], [[@Freeman2017|Freeman (2017)]], [[@Freeman2019|Freeman (2019)]] (revealed-preference axiomatizations of PPE), [[@Masatlioglu2016|Masatlioglu & Raymond (2016)]] (CPE $=$ rank-dependent $\cap$ quadratic), [[@Armouti-Hansen2024b|Armouti-Hansen & Kops (2024)]] (anticipation-managing referents nesting all candidate sources).
- **Applications.** [[@Heidhues2008|Heidhues & Kőszegi (2008)]] (focal/sticky pricing), [[@Koch2011|Koch & Nafziger (2011)]] (goals as self-set referents), [[@Ockenfels2015a|Ockenfels et al. (2015)]] (bonus reference-point violations).

## Cross-paper tensions

The sharpest fault line is **whether the expectations-based referent has any observable footprint at all, and whether the data confirm its sharp comparative statics.** [[@Abeler2011|Abeler et al. (2011)]] manipulate expectations and find exactly the predicted monotone effort response and clustering at the expected payment; [[@Gneezy2017|Gneezy et al. (2017)]], extending the *same* design to $L=0$ and to probability variation that holds the neoclassical incentive fixed, get a *U-shaped* response — falsifying the central monotonicity prediction. The same fragility recurs in the endowment-effect arena: [[@Cerulli-Harms2019|Cerulli-Harms et al. (2019)]] confirm KR's asymmetric comparative static in one experiment and get clean nulls in two others, flipping with presentation order and pricing rule. So one body of evidence treats the model as confirmed, another as refuted, and the refutations are *design-sensitive* rather than decisive.

A second tension is **which solution concept and which referent rule** is the right primitive. KR offer PE, PPE, and CPE; [[@Sprenger2015|Sprenger (2015)]]'s endowment-effect-for-risk data support a *non-equilibrium* "first-focus" referent and reject both disappointment aversion *and* KR's own equilibrium refinements. [[@Baillon2020|Baillon et al. (2020)]] go further: estimating six portable referent rules in one nesting model, they find Status Quo and MaxMin (a security level) dominate at $\sim$60% of subjects, with expectations-based / stochastic rules getting "little support" — a frontal challenge to the now-standard CPE assumption. [[@Armouti-Hansen2024b|Armouti-Hansen & Kops (2024)]] respond by making the referent *any* convex combination of supported outcomes, nesting status-quo, expectations, and narrow-focus dreaming/worrying — but at the cost (their Corollary 5.1) that fully unrestricted formation rationalizes everything, dissolving falsifiability.

Third, an **identification** tension runs through the theory papers. [[@Freeman2017|Freeman (2017)]] shows PPE choice is observationally a Manzini–Mariotti rational shortlist method, so on static riskless data it *cannot* produce attraction or compromise effects; [[@Freeman2016|Freeman (2016)]] and [[@Freeman2019|Freeman (2019)]] show that under risk the footprint is Independence violations (more fundamental than WARP violations). But [[@Masatlioglu2016|Masatlioglu & Raymond (2016)]] prove monotone CPE is behaviorally *identical* to rank-dependent $\cap$ quadratic utility — i.e. correct-beliefs-nonstandard-utility is indistinguishable from distorted-beliefs-standard-utility, and on two-outcome certainty-equivalent menus CPE, Gul, and Bell–Loomes–Sugden all collapse to RDU, so much existing data cannot separate them. Two papers even disagree on what reference dependence *predicts* in the field: [[@Farber2008|Farber (2008)]] shows reference dependence implies a negative labor-supply elasticity only inside an intermediate wage band (not globally), and that estimated daily reference income is too unstable to bind, whereas [[@Crawford2011|Crawford & Meng (2011)]] argue Farber's instability is an artifact of estimating latent targets and that *proxied* rational-expectations targets in both hours and income rescue the model on the same data. And [[@Masatlioglu2016|Masatlioglu & Raymond (2016)]] note monotone linear-gain-loss CPE inherits the Rabin (2000)/Safra–Segal calibration critique unless gain-loss utility is nonlinear — a tension internal to the model's own functional form.

## Open questions

- **What pins down which expectations become the referent?** The "moment of first focus" is underspecified in [[@Koszegi2006b|Kőszegi & Rabin (2006)]]; [[@Gneezy2017|Gneezy et al. (2017)]] conjecture that low/zero payments are simply *ignored* and that residual uncertainty is discouraging, suggesting the available action set and attainable outcome distribution jointly determine the referent — an unmodeled channel.
- **Mean vs. whole-distribution referent.** [[@Abeler2011|Abeler et al. (2011)]] cannot separate a mean-based ([[@Bell1985|Bell]]/[[@Loomes1986|Loomes–Sugden]]/[[@Gul1991|Gul]]) from a distribution-based (KR) referent, and propose a two-fixed-payment lottery as the discriminating test (mean-based predicts a spike at the mean, KR at both payments).
- **Linear vs. nonlinear gain-loss utility.** Only nonlinearity escapes the calibration critique ([[@Masatlioglu2016|Masatlioglu & Raymond 2016]]), but nonlinear CPE loses the RDU toolkit and identification.
- **Why are KR field/lab predictions fragile?** [[@Cerulli-Harms2019|Cerulli-Harms et al. (2019)]] cannot pin down which off-model feature (presentation order, pricing rule) flips support to null — a theory of *when* a referent is adopted is missing.
- **Are WARP violations real?** [[@Freeman2019|Freeman (2019)]] notes the model's permission for WARP violations under risk is empirically *untested*.
- **Heterogeneity of referent rules.** [[@Baillon2020|Baillon et al. (2020)]] and [[@Crawford2011|Crawford & Meng (2011)]] both flag that some agents may be neoclassical, some reference-dependent, calling for individual-level identification (PPE itself is not estimable in the Baillon nesting).

## Candidate ideas

- **A discriminating two-payment effort experiment** implementing [[@Abeler2011|Abeler et al. (2011)]]'s proposed design (lottery over two fixed payments plus accumulated earnings) to test mean-based vs. distribution-based referents, jointly diagnosing the [[@Gneezy2017|Gneezy et al. (2017)]] "$L=0$ is ignored" conjecture by varying whether the low payment is zero.
- **Estimate the [[@Armouti-Hansen2024b|Armouti-Hansen & Kops (2024)]] convex-combination referent on [[@Baillon2020|Baillon et al. (2020)]]-style high-stakes data**, treating the convex weight as the latent rule, to test whether a single mixing parameter beats the six discrete rules and resolves the status-quo-vs-MaxMin-vs-expectations contest.
- **A choice-set-dependent referent model** formalizing the [[@Gneezy2017|Gneezy et al. (2017)]] conjecture that the attainable outcome distribution determines which expectations become referents, axiomatized in the [[@Freeman2017|Freeman (2017)]] / [[@Freeman2019|Freeman (2019)]] revealed-preference framework to keep it falsifiable.
- **A randomization-attitude experiment** exploiting [[@Masatlioglu2016|Masatlioglu & Raymond (2016)]]'s result that mixture aversion ($\lambda \gtrless 1$) is the *only* margin separating CPE from Bell/Loomes–Sugden/Gul — directly eliciting mixing attitudes to adjudicate referent-formation models that all match small-stakes risk aversion.
- **Identify referent heterogeneity in field labor supply** by combining [[@Crawford2011|Crawford & Meng (2011)]]'s proxied-expectations design with a finite-mixture of neoclassical and reference-dependent driver types, testing whether the [[@Farber2008|Farber (2008)]] instability is misspecified homogeneity rather than absent reference dependence.
- **A direct test of EU-PPE WARP violations** under risk, building the menu designs implied by [[@Freeman2019|Freeman (2019)]] (and the random-sales example of [[@Heidhues2008|Heidhues & Kőszegi 2008]]) to check whether the choice-set-level endogenous referent actually breaks WARP in the permitted forms.
