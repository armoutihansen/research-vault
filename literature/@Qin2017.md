> [!abstract] Abstract
> Gerasimou (Econ Theory 61(1):127–145, 2016) characterizes partially dominant choice with acyclic underlying preferences. In this article, we characterize partially dominant choice with transitive underlying preferences. We argue that transitivity is a more appropriate assumption on the underlying preference because the model is interpreted in a way that the deviation from standard rational choice procedures is due to multi-dimensionality and incommensurability of desirable attributes.

## Summary
A focused theory note that re-axiomatizes Gerasimou's (2016) **partially dominant (PD) choice** under a **transitive** underlying preference rather than a merely **acyclic** one. Qin argues that when preference incompleteness arises from multi-dimensional, incommensurable criteria, transitivity is the more appropriate restriction (it is preserved under intersection of criteria), and pins down the single axiom that separates the transitive model (PDT) from the acyclic one (PD).

## Research question
What axioms characterize a partially-dominant choice correspondence when the underlying (possibly incomplete) strict preference $\succ$ is **transitive** instead of only **acyclic** — and which behavioral condition exactly marks PDT off within the broader PD class?

## Method / identification
Setting: a finite set $X$, with $\mathrm{Pow}(X)$ its nonempty subsets, and a choice correspondence $c:\mathrm{Pow}(X)\to\mathrm{Pow}(X)$ satisfying $c(S)\subseteq S$. For an asymmetric relation $\succ$, $c$ is a **partially dominant** procedure if

$$
c(S)=S \iff \bigl[\forall x,y\in S,\ x\not\succ y\bigr], \qquad
c(S)\subsetneq S \iff c(S)=\{x\in S:\ \forall y\in S,\ y\not\succ x \ \wedge\ \exists z\in S,\ x\succ z\},
$$

i.e. chosen alternatives are **undominated** *and* dominate at least one feasible alternative. Gerasimou (2016) treats $\succ$ **acyclic**; Qin requires $\succ$ **transitive** (PDT), motivated by the multi-criteria reading $\succ=\bigcap_{j}\succ_j$ of linear orders — transitivity, unlike acyclicity, survives intersection.

The conditions on $c$ are **a-WARP** (Chernoff / property $\alpha$ restricted to doubletons), **Weak Property $\gamma$**, **Independence of Unselective Choice**, and the new **Elimination by Eliminated**:

$$
\bigl[\,x\notin S,\ S\setminus c(S)\neq\varnothing,\ \forall y\in S\setminus c(S)\ \ c(\{x,y\})=\{x,y\}\,\bigr]\ \Longrightarrow\ x\notin c(S\cup\{x\}).
$$

**Theorem 1**: $c$ is a transitive PD (PDT) procedure **iff** it satisfies a-WARP, Elimination by Eliminated, Weak Property $\gamma$, and Independence of Unselective Choice.

## Data
None — purely axiomatic; four explicit choice correspondences are constructed to prove the axioms are independent.

## Key findings
- **Theorem 1** characterizes PDT, replacing Gerasimou's *Partial b-WARP* with **Elimination by Eliminated**.
- **Lemma 1**: a-WARP $+$ Elimination by Eliminated force the base relation $\succ_c=\{(x,y):c(\{x,y\})=\{x\}\}$ to be **transitive**.
- **Proposition 1 / Corollary 1**: given a-WARP, *Partial b-WARP $\iff$ Weak Elimination $+$ Independence of Unselective Choice*. So it is precisely the strengthening from **Weak Elimination** to **Elimination by Eliminated** that "marks the boundary of PDT procedures within PD procedures."
- Four worked examples establish the logical independence of the four axioms.

## Contribution
Sharpens the axiomatic foundations of incomplete-preference / undominated-choice theory by isolating the **exact behavioral content of transitivity** (vs. acyclicity), and clarifies the logical map between Gerasimou's acyclic model and its transitive refinement.

## Limitations & open questions
- Purely theoretical — **no behavioral/experimental test** of whether observed indecisive choice fits PDT better than PD; the discriminating prediction (the bite of Elimination by Eliminated) is untested.
- Assumes a **finite** $X$ and a single fixed preference; no stochastic, heterogeneous-agent, or menu-frame extension.
- The multi-criteria "intersection of linear orders" motivation is **interpretive** — the criteria $\{\succ_j\}$ are neither modeled as primitives nor identified from choices.
- **Open**: how to empirically separate PDT from PD, and whether the characterization extends to infinite $X$ or to framed/stochastic choice.

## Connections
Directly refines Gerasimou (2016, *Econ. Theory*); sits in the undominated-choice tradition (Sen 1993; Schwartz 1976; Moulin 1985; Bandyopadhyay–Sengupta 1993); built on Chernoff's axiom (property $\alpha$) and property $\gamma$.
