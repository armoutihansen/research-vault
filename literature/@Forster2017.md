## Method / identification
The method is the standard axiomatic-constructive development of measure theory:
- **§§1–3** set up set algebras, rings and $\sigma$-algebras; contents (Inhalte), premeasures (Prämaße) and measures (Maße); and the Carathéodory-style extension of a premeasure to a measure, yielding Lebesgue measure on the Borel $\sigma$-algebra of $\mathbb{R}^n$ generated by the ring of finite box-sums (Quadersummen).
- **§§4–5** define the integral of measurable functions and prove the core convergence/approximation theorems (monotone convergence, Fatou, dominated convergence).
- **§§6–9** establish motion-invariance of Lebesgue measure, the Cavalieri principle and Fubini's theorem on $\mathbb{R}^n$ (deliberately restricted to $\mathbb{R}^n$, foregoing a general product-measure theory), rotationally symmetric functions, and the transformation formula $\int_{T(U)} f\,d\lambda = \int_U (f\circ T)\,|\det DT|\,d\lambda$ for $C^1$ coordinate changes.
- **§§10–13** treat integration by parts, parameter-dependent integrals, the $L^p$-spaces, and Fourier integrals.
- **§§14–17** cover integration on submanifolds, the Gauss (divergence) integral theorem, the potential equation, and distributions (with fundamental solutions for the potential, Helmholtz, and heat equations).
- **§§18–21** introduce Pfaffian forms and curve integrals, higher-order differential forms, integration of differential forms, and culminate in the general Stokes integral theorem on submanifolds of $\mathbb{R}^n$.

## Key findings
The book's "results" are the standard theorems it proves, including:
- Carathéodory extension of a premeasure to a (Lebesgue) measure and construction of Lebesgue measure on $\mathbb{R}^n$ (§3).
- The convergence theorems for the Lebesgue integral — monotone convergence, Fatou's lemma, dominated convergence (§5).
- Motion-invariance of Lebesgue measure (§6) and the Cavalieri principle / **Fubini's theorem** on $\mathbb{R}^n$ (§7).
- The **transformation formula** for integrals under $C^1$ changes of coordinates (§9), foundational for integration on manifolds.
- Completeness and properties of the **$L^p$-spaces** (§12) and the theory of **Fourier integrals** (§13).
- The **Gauss (divergence) theorem** (§15) and treatment of the **potential equation** (§16).
- The theory of **distributions** with fundamental solutions for the potential, Helmholtz, and heat-conduction equations (§17).
- The calculus of **differential forms** and the general **Stokes integral theorem** on submanifolds of $\mathbb{R}^n$ (§§18–21), with applications including the **Brouwer fixed-point theorem** and integral theorems for holomorphic functions of one and several variables.

## Contribution
As a textbook, its contribution is expository and pedagogical: a self-contained, constructive route from set algebras to the general Stokes theorem that consciously trades full generality (general manifolds, abstract product measures) for concreteness on $\mathbb{R}^n$, so students reach computable multidimensional integrals and volumes quickly. It is a long-running classic of the German-language *Aufbaukurs Mathematik* curriculum (the series dates to 1980), continuously revised — this being the 8th, "verbesserte" (improved/corrected) edition, with an errata page maintained on the author's website.
