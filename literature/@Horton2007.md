## Summary
This is a comprehensive beginner-to-intermediate **tutorial textbook on the C++ programming language**, not a research paper. The PDF attached to this Zotero record is in fact *Beginning C++20: From Novice to Professional*, Sixth Edition (Ivor Horton & Peter Van Weert, Apress, 2020, 843 pp.), a revised descendant of Horton's original *Beginning ANSI C++*. It assumes no prior programming knowledge and teaches the essentials of the C++ language and Standard Library through a gradual, hands-on tutorial that emphasizes "modern C++" — newer, safer, more expressive language features (here, those introduced through the C++20 ISO standard) combined with established best practices, stressing not just what one *can* do but what one *should* do.

## Contribution

## Limitations & open questions
The authors explicitly note that **not everything in C++20 is covered** — extensions targeted at more advanced use are deliberately omitted, on the grounds that the full language can no longer be squeezed into a single introductory book. The stated boundary is "the essentials of the C++ language and Standard Library," sufficient to write applications and to extend one's expertise independently afterward. There are no open research problems; the natural "next steps" are advanced/reference texts and the ISO standard itself.
