## Scope

This area covers preferences that depend on *others*: how agents weigh others' material payoffs, repay perceived (un)kindness, manage how they look to an audience, absorb the prescriptions of social categories, and respond to the consumption and behavior of a reference group. Its child topics partition the field by the *primitive* doing the work — the distribution of outcomes ([[inequity-aversion-distributional-preferences|Inequity Aversion & Distributional Social Preferences]]), intentions ([[reciprocity-gift-exchange|Reciprocity, Intentions & Gift Exchange]]), reputation and self-image ([[prosocial-behavior-image-signaling|Prosocial Behavior, Image & Signaling]]), category membership ([[identity-social-norms-conformity|Identity, Social Norms & Conformity]]), task meaning ([[motivated-agents-intrinsic-motivation|Motivated Agents, Mission & Intrinsic Motivation]]), and visible relative position ([[conspicuous-consumption-status|Conspicuous Consumption & Status]]) — while [[peer-effects-social-interactions|Peer Effects & Social Interactions]] is where these forces are *measured* as causal spillovers. Together they ask one question: when is utility a function of more than own payoff, and what is the argument of that function?

## Sub-areas / themes

**The preference primitives (the "what is in the utility function" debate).**
- [[inequity-aversion-distributional-preferences|Inequity Aversion & Distributional Social Preferences]] — outcome-based fairness (Fehr–Schmidt, ERC, Charness–Rabin) and its structural estimation; the field's reference null.
- [[reciprocity-gift-exchange|Reciprocity, Intentions & Gift Exchange]] — the intentions primitive: belief-dependent (un)kindness in psychological games, applied to wage–effort gift exchange.
- [[identity-social-norms-conformity|Identity, Social Norms & Conformity]] — the *social determination* of preferences: category prescriptions $x_C$ entering $U$, and the conformity that propagates them.

**Reputation and meaning (preferences over beliefs about the self).**
- [[prosocial-behavior-image-signaling|Prosocial Behavior, Image & Signaling]] — the signal-extraction logic by which observed good deeds reveal type, and the resulting incentive-crowding fragility.
- [[motivated-agents-intrinsic-motivation|Motivated Agents, Mission & Intrinsic Motivation]] — mission match and intrinsic motivation inside organizations, and crowding-out of effort by control/pay.

**Relative position and its measurement.**
- [[conspicuous-consumption-status|Conspicuous Consumption & Status]] — visible consumption as a costly income signal calibrated to reference-group income; expenditure cascades.
- [[peer-effects-social-interactions|Peer Effects & Social Interactions]] — the empirical-causal layer: identifying endogenous vs. contextual vs. correlated effects under Manski's reflection problem.

## Cross-topic tensions

**Outcomes vs. intentions vs. beliefs — the field's master axis.** What is the *argument* of social utility? [[inequity-aversion-distributional-preferences|Distributional preferences]] make it the payoff vector; [[reciprocity-gift-exchange|reciprocity]] makes it belief-dependent (un)kindness; [[prosocial-behavior-image-signaling|image]] and [[motivated-agents-intrinsic-motivation|motivated-agent]] models make it inferred *type/intent*. The same datum — a worker exerting effort, a donor giving — is fit by all three, and the moonlighting/gift-exchange evidence (Falk–Fehr–Fischbacher, Engelmann–Strobel) rejects the pure-outcome and pure-intention poles together. This is the sharpest tension in the area: the distributional, reciprocity, image, and motivated-agent topics are largely *the same behavioral facts read off four different primitives*, and no design yet cleanly identifies which is load-bearing in a given setting.

**Who is the reference agent / reference group?** Every topic leans on an unmodeled comparator and none derives it. [[inequity-aversion-distributional-preferences|Inequity aversion]] defaults to the egalitarian split (challenged by rank and identity); [[reciprocity-gift-exchange|gift exchange]] needs a wage reference standard (fixed vs. adaptive $w_{t-1}$); [[conspicuous-consumption-status|conspicuous consumption]] assumes a common reference-income distribution (which fails within-group); [[identity-social-norms-conformity|identity]] supplies the categories that *define* the group. [[peer-effects-social-interactions|Peer effects]] then shows the relevant comparator is empirically unsettled (room vs. dorm, top vs. mean vs. next-door). The reference-group problem is the single unsolved object cutting across all seven.

**Visibility crowds in or crowds out — and through which channel?** [[prosocial-behavior-image-signaling|Image]] (signal dilution), [[motivated-agents-intrinsic-motivation|motivated agents]] (signaling about type/norm vs. reciprocity vs. esteem), and [[conspicuous-consumption-status|status]] (visibility raises display) all hinge on observability, yet disagree on its sign and mechanism. Observability is simultaneously the field's favorite identification lever and the reason self-image can never be separated from social signaling.

**Stable preference vs. emergent equilibrium object.** Are these dispositions fixed agent characteristics (the structural-types program of [[inequity-aversion-distributional-preferences|distributional preferences]]) or endogenous to history and equilibrium ([[identity-social-norms-conformity|identity]]'s enculturated actor, [[conspicuous-consumption-status|cascades]], [[peer-effects-social-interactions|social multipliers]])? A "die-hard fixed-preference" reading can recast any norm/identity result as Bayesian updating — an identification escape no topic closes.

## Open questions

- **Can a tractable, falsifiable model fuse distribution, intentions, and beliefs?** Hybrids fit but are parameter-heavy and equilibrium-bound; the area lacks a portable, out-of-sample-testable unifier across the [[inequity-aversion-distributional-preferences|distributional]] and [[reciprocity-gift-exchange|reciprocity]] topics.
- **How are social-comparison groups formed and prescriptions $x_C$ generated?** Flagged as unsolved by every topic that uses a reference object; sharpened to "infinite and not well-defined" in [[identity-social-norms-conformity|identity]].
- **Why does observed prosociality fail to transfer across domains?** Lab-prosocial types do not reciprocate in the field ([[reciprocity-gift-exchange|gift exchange]]); type taxonomies are barely tested cross-game ([[inequity-aversion-distributional-preferences|distributional preferences]]); self- vs. social-image is unidentified ([[prosocial-behavior-image-signaling|image]]).
- **Beliefs assumed vs. measured.** Imposing rational expectations distorts recovered social preferences and reframes "preference failures" as belief failures (mispricing employers, naive impact beliefs) — a methodological tension spanning the structural-estimation strands of the whole area.
- **From individual disposition to aggregate spillover.** Whether peer/status multipliers are symmetric, dynamic, and linear-in-means is open ([[peer-effects-social-interactions|peer effects]]), and whether ingroup salience can tip coordination to efficient equilibria is untested ([[identity-social-norms-conformity|identity]]).
